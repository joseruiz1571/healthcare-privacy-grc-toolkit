# ADDENDUM: Acceptable Use of Generative AI

**Subject:** Acceptable Use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) Tools
**Applies To:** All Workforce Members, Contractors, and Business Associates
**Effective Date:** [Current Date]
**Reference:** HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E)

---

## 1. Policy Statement

[Organization] recognizes the potential of Generative AI (GenAI) to enhance productivity and innovation. However, the use of these tools introduces specific risks regarding data privacy, accuracy, and intellectual property. This policy addendum establishes mandatory requirements for the safe and compliant use of GenAI tools within the organization.

**All workforce members are prohibited from inputting, pasting, or uploading Protected Health Information (PHI) or Confidential Business Information into any public, non-approved GenAI tool.**

---

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Generative AI (GenAI)** | Artificial intelligence systems capable of generating text, images, or other media in response to prompts (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Midjourney). |
| **Public/Open Models** | GenAI tools that are freely available or accessible via personal accounts, where user input may be used to train the model. |
| **Enterprise/Closed Models** | GenAI tools procured by the organization under a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) where data privacy and non-retention are contractually guaranteed. |

---

## 3. Requirements and Guidelines

### 3.1 Data Privacy and PHI

- **Strict Prohibition:** Users must NEVER enter PHI (names, medical record numbers, diagnoses, treatment notes) into a public GenAI tool. Doing so constitutes an unauthorized disclosure to a third-party vendor (the AI provider) and is a potential HIPAA breach.
- **De-Identification Is Insufficient:** Even if direct identifiers are removed, the combination of clinical details may still render the data identifiable. Do not use public tools for clinical scenarios.

### 3.2 Approved Tools

Workforce members may only use GenAI tools that have been:

1. Vetted by Information Security and Compliance.
2. Approved for specific use cases.
3. Covered by a valid Business Associate Agreement (BAA) if PHI is involved.

> A list of approved tools is maintained on the Compliance Intranet Page.

### 3.3 Accuracy and Human Oversight

- **Verification:** GenAI tools can generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information. Users are responsible for verifying the accuracy of all AI-generated content.
- **Clinical Decision Support:** GenAI outputs regarding patient care, coding, or billing must be reviewed and validated by a qualified human professional before being entered into the medical record or used for decision-making.

---

## 4. Scenarios

| Scenario | Verdict | Explanation |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Drafting a Policy:** An admin uses a public AI tool to draft a generic office holiday schedule with no names or sensitive data. | ✅ Permitted | No PHI or confidential business strategy is shared. |
| **Summarizing Notes:** A nurse pastes patient progress notes into a public AI tool to "make it more concise" for the chart. | ❌ Violation | The input data (PHI) is now stored on an external server without a BAA. This is a reportable incident. |
| **Coding Assistance:** A coder asks an AI, "What is the ICD-10 code for [Specific Condition]?" without identifying a patient. | ⚠️ Caution | Permitted if no patient specifics are shared, but the code must be verified against official coding references. |
| **Research Summary:** A physician uses a public AI tool to summarize a published medical journal article with no patient information. | ✅ Permitted | Published research is not PHI. Output accuracy must still be verified. |

---

## 5. Reporting and Non-Compliance

- **Incident Reporting:** If a user accidentally inputs PHI into a non-approved AI tool, they must immediately report the incident to the Privacy Officer or via the designated reporting line to allow for mitigation (e.g., data deletion requests to the vendor).
- **Disciplinary Action:** Failure to adhere to this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

---

## 6. Review

This addendum will be reviewed annually or as needed to reflect changes in technology, regulation, or organizational risk posture.
